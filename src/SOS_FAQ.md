# SOS Emergency Alert System - Frequently Asked Questions

## üì± For Citizens

### General Questions

**Q: What is the SOS Emergency Alert System?**
A: It's a feature that allows BantayAlert users to send emergency distress signals directly to emergency responders with one click. When you're in danger, you can alert authorities about your situation, location, and contact information instantly.

**Q: When should I use the SOS Alert?**
A: Use the SOS Alert when:
- You're in immediate danger but can't make a phone call
- You need emergency evacuation assistance
- You're trapped or stranded
- You witness a disaster and need help
- You need to notify authorities of your situation quickly

**Q: When should I NOT use the SOS Alert?**
A: Do NOT use for:
- Non-emergency situations
- Testing the system (use demo mode if available)
- False alarms or pranks
- Situations where you can safely call 911 directly

**Q: Does the SOS Alert replace calling 911?**
A: No! For immediate life-threatening emergencies, always call 911 first. The SOS Alert is meant to supplement emergency services, not replace them. Use it when you can't make a call or need to send location data quickly.

---

### Using the SOS System

**Q: Do I need to be signed in to send an SOS Alert?**
A: Yes, you must be signed in to send SOS Alerts. This ensures emergency responders can identify you and contact you back.

**Q: How do I send an SOS Alert?**
A: 
1. Open BantayAlert and sign in
2. Go to your Dashboard
3. Click the red "SEND SOS ALERT" button
4. Describe your emergency
5. Click "Send SOS Alert"

**Q: What information is sent with my SOS Alert?**
A: Your alert includes:
- Your name and email
- Your phone number (if in your profile)
- Your GPS location (if available)
- Your emergency description
- Date and time of alert

**Q: What if I don't have location services enabled?**
A: You can still send an SOS Alert. It will include all your other information, but responders won't have your exact coordinates. We strongly recommend enabling location for faster response.

**Q: Can I send an SOS Alert without a phone number?**
A: Yes, but it's not recommended. Emergency responders need a way to contact you. Please add your phone number in your Profile settings.

**Q: What happens after I send an SOS Alert?**
A: 
1. You'll see a confirmation message
2. Emergency departments receive your alert immediately
3. They can see your location, contact info, and situation
4. Responders will contact you and/or dispatch help
5. You'll be contacted by emergency services

**Q: Can I cancel an SOS Alert if I sent it by accident?**
A: You should immediately call the emergency hotline (911) and explain it was sent by mistake. The department can mark it as cancelled, but you should still notify them directly.

**Q: How long does it take for departments to see my alert?**
A: Departments see your alert immediately. Their dashboard auto-refreshes every 30 seconds, so your alert will appear within moments.

**Q: Will I know when someone responds to my alert?**
A: Emergency responders will contact you directly via phone or may arrive at your location. Make sure your phone is on and accessible.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Does the SOS Alert work without internet?**
A: No, you need an internet connection to send an SOS Alert. If you have no internet, call 911 directly.

**Q: Will the SOS Alert work on my mobile device?**
A: Yes, BantayAlert works on all modern smartphones, tablets, and computers with internet access.

**Q: Why can't I click the SOS button?**
A: The button is disabled when you're not signed in. Please sign in first, then try again.

**Q: My location isn't being detected. What should I do?**
A: 
1. Check if your browser has location permissions enabled
2. Try enabling location services on your device
3. If it still doesn't work, you can still send the alert - just mention your location in the description

**Q: Is my information secure?**
A: Yes, all data is transmitted securely and only accessible by authorized emergency responders. We take your privacy and security seriously.

### Using the Department Dashboard

**Q: How often are alerts updated?**
A: The dashboard automatically refreshes every 30 seconds to show new alerts. You can also click the "Refresh" button anytime.

**Q: What do the different priority levels mean?**
A: 
- üî¥ **Critical**: Life-threatening, immediate response required
- üü† **High**: Urgent assistance needed
- üü° **Medium**: Important but not immediately life-threatening

**Q: What do the different statuses mean?**
A:
- **Active**: New alert, needs response
- **Responding**: Team has been dispatched
- **Resolved**: Assistance provided, situation handled
- **Cancelled**: False alarm or duplicate alert

**Q: How do I respond to an alert?**
A:
1. Review the alert details
2. Click "Call Citizen" to contact them
3. Click "Dispatch Team" to mark as responding
4. Click "Navigate" to get directions
5. Click "Mark Resolved" when complete

**Q: Can I update an alert after someone else has responded?**
A: Yes, multiple responders can see and update the same alert. The system tracks who responded most recently.

**Q: What if I accidentally dispatch to the wrong alert?**
A: You can update the status back to "Active" or contact your dispatch coordinator. Always verify the alert details before dispatching.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Why am I seeing "Unauthorized" errors?**
A: 
- Verify you're using correct department credentials
- Check that you selected "Department" user type

- Contact support if issue persists

**Q: The alerts aren't loading. What should I do?**
A:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Refresh the page
3. Clear your browser cache
4. Try signing out and back in
5. Contact technical support

**Q: Can I export alert data for reports?**
A: This feature is planned for a future update. Currently, you can view all alert history in the "All Alerts" view.

**Q: How long are alerts stored in the system?**
A: All alerts are stored indefinitely in the system for record-keeping and analysis.

## üîß Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Q: "Failed to send SOS" error message**
A: Try these steps:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Refresh the page and try again
3. Make sure you're signed in
4. If problem persists, call 911 directly

**Q: Location shows as "unavailable"**
A: 
1. Enable location services in your browser
2. Grant location permission when prompted
3. Try sending alert anyway (location helps but isn't required)

**Q: I didn't receive a confirmation**
A: 
- Check if you saw a success toast notification
- Look at your activity log to see if it was recorded
- If unsure, call 911 to verify

**Q: The button isn't clickable**
A: Make sure you're signed in. The button is disabled for unsigned users.

**Q: Alerts aren't appearing in department dashboard**
A: 
- Wait 30 seconds for auto-refresh
- Click the Refresh button manually
- Check your department credentials
- Verify internet connection
